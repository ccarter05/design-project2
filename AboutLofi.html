What is Lo-fi?


According to Masterclass.com, “Lo-fi, or lofi, refers to a recording with less than professional
or flawed sound quality. “Lo-fi” is short for “low fidelity,” and initially referred to a low-quality
recording with audible imperfections, such as background noise or performance mistakes” (Masterclass.com).
Lo-fi is in simple terms music that has purposeful mistakes or imperfections that make the music more nostalgic
or de-stressing.

Why people like lofi


Lofi is a unique genre of music that most people don’t know. It is, however, getting popular
in streaming. The music is so relaxing and simple that more and more people are starting to
love it even more. The music has a distinct sound and experimental nature that people love.
It is very much like what is called low-fidelity. It has background sound that other popular
songs would remove such as rain, fire, wind, footsteps, etc. It welcomes the imperfection in a
way that pleases the listener's ears. It has also become popular as a studying tool (later explained).
